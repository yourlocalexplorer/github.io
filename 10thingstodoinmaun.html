7  THINGS TO DO IN MAUN 

Why cross the border when you have your own haven in your backyard? 

With Independence and December approaching and our borders remaining closed we have taken the responsibility to give Batswana destinations choices with affordable activities that they will love during the festive period.
This article will highlight 7 activities locals can do in Maun.

1. Luna Bar

If you love meat and a chilled spot to have your drinks, you can consider a session at Luna Bar. Luna Bar is located in the heart of Maun in New Town where you’ll hear the most vibrant modern music mixed with the open minded crowd.

Luna Bar has a butchery with already out of this world marinated meat which you could give to the Braai around the corner to prepare for you while you sit back and relax. If you wish for an exclusive feel there, you can pay to get into the VIP section. This place is a must visit location. 

2. A boat cruise into the Boro river 

I’m sure we all have watched a movie where we see a couple or a family cruising along river having the time of the lives. In Maun we have the same opportunity for under P200 per person. Imagine waking up on a Saturday morning with nothing to do and your family decide to spend the afternoon a chilled location by the Boro river with a Braai stand and chilled vibes. There are countless Boats to pick for a cruise but it if you wish to go on a cruise follow the link.

3. Scenic Flights.
To understand the value of the scenic flight you need to understand that Botswana has the only in land delta with such a beautiful diversified range of game animals living amongst themselves. Local charter companies have offered special rates for 20min to 1hr scenic flights over the Delta for under P1000 per person. Once you’ve seen the Detla from above you would have experienced a view that thousands of people around the world spend their savings just to see. 

4. Mobile Safari

Now, for those of you who don’t want to fly, you have the opportunity to do a day trip or mobile safari into the delta. This includes a 6 seater vehicle with one meal (depending on the package you request). Most affordable Mobile Safari’s can charged under P1000 per person and in the case of spending a night at a camp site will require a quote with includes of your choice. 

5. Eating out at a restaurants or lodges in Maun 

There’s no substitution for a river side meal in Maun with the perfect lighting and soothing sound of jazz playing in the back. Maun restaurants and lodges have some of the most mind blowing views which would drop any jaw. The food and beverages at most of the lodges are affordable for the average local and they do offer a diverse menu for any diet and craving. If you wish to find any the restaurants you can google the nearest lodge. 

6. Horse Riding. 

If you’re a group, family, couple or a solo ranger, going horse riding is something you should really consider. For only P200 per person per hour, Royal Tree Lodge offers an out of this world experience where you get the opportunity to move through the thamalakane river on the most majestic well trained horses while viewing some of the most amazing game close by.

7. Visiting the New Old Bridge

Maun has years of history attached to it and one of its most iconic features is the Old Bridge. The Old Bridge has been rejuvenated within the past 2 years and has become a stunning new sight you where you could take your family, pets and self (depending on the mood).
