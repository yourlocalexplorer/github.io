Why learn a foreign Language

The world is becoming a smaller place due to technology, why not learn each others languages?

Covid 19 changed how commerce and basic life operates all over the world. We’ve been told to distance ourselves from each other and to spend about three-month quarantining in the comfort of our own homes. Despite the above difficulties the world became a smaller place due to technology. We witnessed social platforms go from a source of entertainment to becoming a beckon of hope for the world. With technology bringing us closer, we noticed that this could be an opportunity for Batswana to learn new languages and cultures which we could later use in business and in our way of living. 

Botswana’s highest functioning industries are the Diamond and Tourism sectors. Both industries are dependent on foreign trade/interaction in order to prosper and this leads to the topic we wish to address in this article. Majority of our international clients who either visit Botswana to see the amazing camps/lodges or attend diamond auctions come from countries where English is a second language. For us to communicate more effectively with these potential clients, it may be in our best interest to learn UN Languages such as German, French and Spanish. 

With the world becoming closer through Technology, it allows us Batswana to Market our products and services more effectively. Communicating in the same language as our potential customers, gives you a competitive edge over your competitors and also increases your chances of being employed in industries that deal with clients from these countries.
